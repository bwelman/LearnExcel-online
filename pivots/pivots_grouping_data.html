Grouping data

Grouping data in a PivotTable can help you to show a subset of your data to analyze. This is especially important when you want to group a list of dates into years, quarters or months. Grouping is used frequently in many business scenarios.

Automatic time grouping is a new feature Excel 2016. Time-related fields are automatically detected and grouped. This automatic grouping creates new relevant Date/Time columns such as Years, Quarters and Months and automatically populates the PivotTable. In the example above, the quarter and month are no fields in the original source data.

Which date/time columns are added depends on the granularity of the dates/times in the data table. For example, if the dates are in days and is for more than a year’s duration, then the added grouping columns would be for months, quarters and years.

Once the Date/Time columns are added to the model you can quickly start analyzing the data across the different levels of time with drill-down capabilities. This way you may find additional insights. For example, to get a monthly year-over-year comparison of your data, you can move the Years field to the COLUMNS area while keeping only the Months field in the ROWS area.

The + (plus) button in the PivotTable indicates a collapsed level. Clicking on it expand all elements in the PivotTable to the next level. This feature is called drill-down.

Changing the Group columns is always possible through the usual user interface. In the PivotTable, right-click the Date column and select Group. In the Group dialog box that appears, you can add or delete other time levels.

When you don’t like the automatic grouping, you can always undo this like other changes. The first undo (Ctrl+Z) removes the auto-added columns from the ROWS area. The second undo removes the newly added columns from the model. In case these fields are already used in another PivotTable, then they will not be deleted from the model. Right-click Ungroup… is also supported and will act as if you have done the two undo operations, removing the group fields from the areas and the model.
