Task: Example Grouping

An example of grouping data based on a date field.

The practice file contains a data table named SalesData and columns Region, Salesman, InvoiceNr, InvoiceDate, ProductCategory, and InvoiceAmount.

File: Invoices.xlsx
  1. Open the practice file.
First of all, a PivotTable with the sales per salesman per product category is created.
  1. Select any cell in the table. Choose tab Insert > PivotTable (group Tables).

    The dialog box Create PivotTable appears. The range of the table has already filled in.

  2. In this task, the PivotTable should appear in a new worksheet. So accept the default location and click OK.
  3. Drag field Salesman to the area Rows, field Productcategory to the area Columns, and field InvoiceAmount to the area Values. Give the values a currency format.

    Note that there are big differences in sales per salesman.

  4. Drag field InvoiceDate to the area Rows and below field Salesman.
    Excel's automatic time grouping feature creates two calculated fields, Years and Quarters in the Rows area.
    And in the PivotTable, the sales are grouped per year.

    You now see that Brown started selling in 2011 and not in 2010, which is a possible explanation for the lower sales.

  5. Click on the + (plus) button in front of 2010 of Anderson. And after that on the + button in front of Qtr1.
    First the levels of the years 2010 are expanded tot quarters. After that, the Qtr1 levels are expanded to the months.
  6. Click on the - (minus) buttons to collapse all levels.
  7. Drag field Productcategory out of the Columns area. Drag field Quarters from Rows to Columns. Drag fields Years and InvoiceDate out of the Rows area.
To easily compare values you can also choose for displaying percentages (of row total, column total ,or grand total).
  1. Select a numeric value in the PivotTable and choose tab Analyze > Field Settings (group Active Field). In the dialog box Value Field Settings select tab Show values As and select % of Row Total.
  2. Click OK.

Data analyze questions

Use the PivotTable possibilities to answer the following questions. There are multiple ways to find the answers.

  1. Which salesman has the greatest total sales in December 2010?
  2. In which month of which year were the maximum sales of software?
  3. What are the sales percentages for hard- and software per region?
  4. In which quarter of which year has Anderson the lowest sales?
  5. Analyze the sales of Brown in quarter 2 of 2011. Do you notice anything?
