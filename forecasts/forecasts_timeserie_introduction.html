What is a time serie?

The historical data are usually time series, a set of observations of a variable measured at successive intervals or over successive periods. When the data provided have a specific pattern in the time, then can this pattern can be used to make a forecast for a future value.

It could also be that the value of a variable depends on other variables than the time. For example, there could be a relation between blood pressure and age. You then have an independent variable (age) and a dependent variable (blood pressure). Such relationships between variables are examined withregression-analysis. This chapter discusses only forecasts with time series. When you see the time as an independent variable, then you can also use regression analysis in time series.

After the study and analysis of time series, models with associated forecasting techniques are prepared. Often you try multiple models with an accuracy analysis. The cost of inaccurate predictions can be significant. Over-optimistic forecasts can result in the production of too many items that might be destroyed. And low forecasts can result in lost revenue opportunities.

Depending on the amount of data, the desired quality of the forecasts and the presence of specific statistical know-how, the calculations will be done with statistical software packages (R, SPSS, ...) or with Microsoft Excel. In this chapter you will only work with the standard possibilities of Excel, without specific add-inns. A single time the result of Excel is compared to the result that R provides.

A time series is a set of (measure) data of a variable over a certain period of time. The data consists of repeated measurements at fixed times such as each hour day week, month, quarter, year or regular period. The data is thus ordered in time and the time between each observation is constant.

Examples of time series:
  • Sickness absence per month or year
  • Sales per month
  • Sales per quarter
  • Road deaths per year
  • …

Many time series show a trend (increase or decrease) and / or seasonality, variations specific to a particular time frame. For example the sale of ice in summer will be higher than in winter.

When making forecasts you want to estimate future values ​​of the series. For this you use data from the past. These historical data are studied and analyzed trying to detect a pattern in the data to make the best possible model of the time series.
