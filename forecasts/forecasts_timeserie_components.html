Time-serie components

In this section, four types of patterns in time-series are discussed.

Trend component

Tend to ascend over time (positive trend) or descending(negative trend). It does not have to be linear. Sometimes only a trend referred to as the direction (ascending or descending) in which the changes take place.

Seasonality component

These are regular fluctuations per day, month, quarter (usually within one year). Sales of ice creams in the summer will be higher than in the winter. Seasonality always concerns a fixed, known period.

Cyclic component

These are fluctuations over a longer period, often for many years, through fluctuations in the business cycle. Characteristic is also that these periods have no fixed length. It can be omitted for analysis on shorter periods. The business cycle is a combination of different factors or indicators.

Random component

These are irregular fluctuations that cannot be explained. Also called accidental influences.
