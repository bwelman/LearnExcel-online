Task: Changing chart location

A chart on a worksheet can easily be moved to another location.

You can create a chart on the same worksheet as the data. This is a embedded chart. You can also display add a chart to a separate sheet that only contains a chart, a chart sheet. An embedded chart on a worksheet can be moved by dragging it to another place, but you can also move it to a chart sheet. Both methods are practiced in this task.

File: Bakery_Chart.xlsx
  1. Open the practice file.
  2. Select the chart by clicking within the chart area.
    A double border around the chart appears and the mouse cursor has been changed to a cross with arrows .
  3. Press the left mouse button and drag the chart to the desired place.
    Tip: If you hold down the Alt key while dragging the chart will snap to the gridlines of the worksheet.
  4. Click outside the chart in the worksheet to deselect.
  5. Select the chart again and choose Design > Move Chart (group Location).
  6. Select New sheet, give the name for the new sheet, then click OK.
    The chart sheet is displayed.

Now move the chart back to worksheet.

  1. Select the chart in the chart sheet.
  2. Choose Design > Move Chart (group Location).
    The dialog box Move chart appears.
  3. Select Object in, and choose the worksheet on which the chart should be embedded, and then click OK.
    The worksheet with the embedded chart is shown and the chart sheet has been deleted.
