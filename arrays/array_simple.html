Task: Simple Array Formulas

Learn how to create simple array formulas.

In the next figure, you can see that the sum and product of two numbers are calculated a few times. This can be done with simple formulas. For example, the formula in D2 could be =A2+B2. And the formula in E2 could be =A2*B2. When copying these formulas down the correct formulas are also in D3:E4.

This calculation can also be done with array formulas. This provides no benefits here, but it is a good task to learn to work with array formulas.

File: Array1.xlsx
  1. Open the practice file.
  2. Select area D2:D4 and type =A2:A4+B2:B4 and then press CTRL SHIFT ENTER.
    Because the result of the addition is a column of three numbers, you should first select an area of three cells for the results.

    The results you see in the cells D2:D4. On the formula bar, you see Excel surrounded the formula with braces.

  3. Select area E2:E4 and type =A2:A4*B2:B4 and then press CTRL SHIFT ENTER.

    The results you see in the cells E2:E4. On the formula bar, you see Excel surrounded the formula with braces.
