Task: Calculate sellings

A step by step explanation of a simple example with formulas.

This is an exercise for a very simple calculation, see the figure below. Three cells contain numbers:
  • C1, the purchase amount.
  • B2, the profit rate.
  • B5, the VAT rate.
Four cells (C2, C3, C5 and C6) contain formulas. The idea is that if you change one or more of the values in C1, B2 or B5 all the depending values are automatically adjusted.
  1. Start with a new worksheet in a new workbook.
  2. Type the texts you see in column A.
    Some texts don't fit within the column width. That is not important at the moment. If desired, you can make the column wider.
  3. In cell B2 enter the value 25%, in cell B5 the value 21% and in cell C1 the value 4000.
  4. Enter in cell C2 the formula =B2*C1.
    After pressing the Enter key, in cell C2 the result of the formula appears: 1000. This is the calculated profit.
  5. Enter in cell C3 the formula =C1+C2.
    Upon completion of the input in cell C3 the result appears: 5000.
  6. Enter in cell C5 the formula =B5*C3.
    After completion in cell C5 the result appears: 1050.
  7. Enter in cell C6 the formula =C3+C5.
    After completion in cell C6 the result appears: 6050.
  8. Experiment with other values for purchase, profit rate and VAT rate.
    Note that by changing the values, all formulas will be automatically recalculated.
